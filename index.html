Publications

Passenger-Oriented Intelligent Suspension with a Biodynamic Model and Skyhook Control

21 November 2021 | Proceedings of the Latin American Congress on Automation and Robotics

Abstract

Intelligent suspension systems assist in decreasing the impact of road disturbances on passenger comfort, and its design requires a mathematical analysis that includes the elements of the suspension and the passenger’s vertical dynamics. The contribution of this research is to merge a 4 degrees-of-freedom (DOF) Boileau-Rakheja biodynamic passenger model with a 3-DOF suspension model to generate more accurate representations of the displacements and accelerations of the main passenger’s masses. Moreover, the contribution is complemented with a Skyhook controller in a semi-active 7-DOF one-quarter vehicle suspension to improve passenger comfort, and a case study presents tests in frequency and time domains. The analysis was conducted by establishing a regulatory closed-loop feedback control for a state-space system, simulating its behavior using MATLAB/Simulink, and comparing the results against specific performance criteria. The active suspension’s frequency response showed a 40% reduction in the displacement transmissibility of the suspended masses at low frequencies (0–4 Hz), but exhibited a 2–10% increase in the rms acceleration at high frequencies (4–15 Hz). The herein results could be the base for further studies in the passenger comfort and road holding for one quarter, but also for half and full vehicle.

http://dx.doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-90033-5_25

Computing and Assessment of Discrete Angle Positions for Optimizing the Solar Energy Harvesting for Urban Sustainable Development

9 Octuber 2021 | MDPI energies

Abstract

This paper proposes the computation and assessment of optimal tilt and azimuth angles for a receiving surface, using a mathematical model developed at the University of Tomsk, Russia. The model was validated and analyzed for the Nuevo León State, Northeast Mexico, utilizing a set of metrics, comparing against satellite data from NASA. A point of interest in the city of Monterrey was analyzed to identify orientation patterns throughout the year for an optimal solar energy gathering. The aim is providing the best orientation tilt angles for photovoltaic or solar thermal panels without tracking systems. In addition, this analysis is proposed as a tool to achieve optimal performance in sustainable urban development in the region. Based on the findings, a set of optimal tilt and azimuth surface angles are proposed for the analyzed coordinates. The aim is to identify the optimal performance to obtain the maximum solar irradiation possible over the year for solar projects in the region. The results show that the model can be used as a tool to accelerate decision making in the design of solar harvesting surfaces and allows the design of discrete tracking systems with an increase in solar energy harvesting above 5% annually.

https://doi.org/10.3390/en14206441

Projects

Industrial robotics

2022

The Final Project in Industrial Robotics focused on utilizing ROS (Robot Operating System) for a multi-rover system. The project involved applying concepts such as services, workspaces, packages, topics, and nodes. The goal was to have four rovers, with three performing exploration tasks and one acting as a monitor. The project used ROS2 version Humble Hawksbill and the TurtleSim simulator for testing. The main tasks included opening the simulator, clearing the ocean, positioning the rovers, initiating exploration and monitoring sequences, concluding monitoring and shutdown sequences, and returning a rover to its base. The project aimed to demonstrate the practical application of ROS in robotic exploration and monitoring, showcasing the integration of services, topics, and simulation for effective rover coordination.

The goal was to optimize the process by moving bulk oranges, inspecting and removing non-conforming fruits, and packaging them in boxes. The project recognized the importance of the Mexican orange industry, highlighting the opportunity for improvement in this sector. By leveraging automation and robotics, the project aimed to enhance productivity and efficiency in orange processing..

Actuators

2021

The objective of this project was to design a servo system capable of moving a mechanical load and measuring its key variables, including current, position, and velocity. Through the design of a 1:16 gear set, we successfully increased the torque to achieve the desired performance. In terms of results, we successfully developed the servo system and conducted tests to determine important values. We utilized SolidWorks for designing the necessary parts, which were then 3D printed for implementation. Additionally, we implemented a methodology to measure changes in current and conducted various tests within MATLAB. The analysis without load provided valuable insights into the position, velocity, and current characteristics of the system. Furthermore, we obtained results for the analysis with load, allowing us to evaluate the performance under realistic conditions.

Applied Electronic

2021

Electromyograms are analyses of the muscular behavior of an individual based on the electric charges emitted by their muscles. These charges are typically very small and noisy, making it necessary to use signal conditioners to obtain valuable information from them. In this project, three types of signal conditioning are employed: amplification, rectification, and signal filtering. In addition to that, through "Plug and Play," the user can utilize the raw signal, a 60 Hz bandstop suppressor, a -20 dB low-pass filter, or a -60 dB Butterworth low-pass filter. All of this is achieved using operational amplifiers and a printed circuit board (PCB) designed in EasyEDA.

Industrial Informatics

2019

Our project consisted of designing and implementing a structure, whether for work or housing, with the aim of conserving energy and maximizing its use. Additionally, we integrated features that promote water conservation and the health of its occupants. Our solution relies on an array of sensors that regulate the use of energy and water. For example, motion and photovoltaic sensors control the lighting system, while water sensors measure the garden's water levels and the water tank. We also incorporated a fan that operates based on temperature readings and a servomotor that manages the water tank's movement. All these functions work automatically to ensure energy efficiency, but we also provided an option for manual control by the user. Our project was implemented using Arduino and LabVIEW, two widely used platforms for developing and prototyping control systems. Our motivation for this project stems from the realization that energy consumption is a fundamental issue that cannot be ignored. While electricity is indispensable, we recognize that its misuse contributes to unnecessary expenditures. As a result, our objective was to create a housing structure that promotes maximum energy usage while minimizing energy expenses.
